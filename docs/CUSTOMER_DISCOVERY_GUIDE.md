# Customer Discovery Guide

**Objective**: Validate market need and customer willingness to pay  
**Duration**: 20-30 minute discovery calls  
**Target**: 20 calls in Phase 17B

---

## Utility Executive Discovery Script

**Target Persona**: Chief Planner, VP Engineering, VP Operations (utilities)

### Opening (2 min)

"Thanks for taking the time. I know you're busy, so I'll be brief. We built a tool that helps utilities plan capacity and interconnection faster. I wanted to learn more about how you handle planning today and see if this might be relevant."

### Problem Discovery (8-10 min)

**Question 1: Current Planning Process**
> "Walk me through how you handle capacity planning right now. From the moment you know there's a new interconnection request or load growth, to when you finalize what upgrades are needed—what does that look like?"

**Listen for**:
- ✅ Timeline (how many weeks/months?)
- ✅ People involved (how many FTEs?)
- ✅ Tools used (manual spreadsheets? specialized software?)
- ✅ Bottlenecks ("This step always takes forever...")
- ✅ Pain points ("We never have enough detail by the time we need it...")

**Question 2: Interconnection Challenges**
> "What about interconnection requests? When a developer comes to you asking for 100 MW or 500 MW, how long does it typically take to assess feasibility and provide an offer?"

**Listen for**:
- ✅ Actual timeline (days? weeks? months?)
- ✅ Queue backlog ("We have 50+ waiting...")
- ✅ Frustration level
- ✅ Revenue impact ("Faster decisions = more revenue")

**Question 3: Current Problems**
> "If I could wave a magic wand and solve one problem in your planning process, what would it be?"

**Listen for**:
- ✅ Urgency signals (speed, accuracy, visibility)
- ✅ Financial impact ("...which costs us $X when it's slow")
- ✅ Strategic priority ("Our board is pushing us to...")

**Question 4: Decision Making**
> "When it comes to new tools or capabilities, who would be involved in the decision? And what would need to be true for you to move forward?"

**Listen for**:
- ✅ Buying committee (just you? finance? ops?)
- ✅ Budget availability ("We have $X allocated...")
- ✅ Decision timeline ("By Q3...")
- ✅ Approval criteria ("It needs to integrate with...")

### Qualification (5 min)

**Question 5: Capability Assessment**
> "Let me describe what we do in 30 seconds. [30-second pitch]. Does that sound like it could help with what you mentioned?"

**If YES, continue:**

> "What would you need to see to believe it would actually work for your specific situation?"

**Listen for**:
- ✅ Willingness to pilot
- ✅ Specific scenarios they want tested
- ✅ Success criteria ("If you can model X scenario in < 2 hours...")

**If NO or UNCERTAIN:**

> "What would need to be different? Is it a timing thing, or does this just not solve the right problem?"

**Listen for**:
- ✅ Real objections (worth addressing) vs. polite dismissal
- ✅ Alternative solutions they're already using

### Close (3-5 min)

**If Qualified**:
> "I'd love to run a quick demo showing exactly what this looks like with a scenario similar to yours. Are you open to 30 minutes next week?"

**If Not Qualified**:
> "I appreciate the candor. If the priorities change and you're looking to accelerate planning, I'll reach out in a few months. Sound good?"

## Data Center Developer Discovery Script

**Target Persona**: VP Development, Chief Infrastructure Officer, VP Grid Planning (developers)

### Opening (2 min)

"We help data centers and large industrial facilities navigate grid interconnection faster. I know permitting and interconnection can be slow, so I wanted to understand your process and see if we could help."

### Problem Discovery (8-10 min)

**Question 1: Interconnection Experience**
> "Walk me through your last major data center project—from when you identified the site to when you had an interconnection agreement signed. How long did that take?"

**Listen for**:
- ✅ Timeline (6 months? 18 months?)
- ✅ Frustration points ("The utility took forever to assess...")
- ✅ Alternatives considered ("We looked at 3 different sites...")
- ✅ Revenue impact ("Each month of delay costs us $X...")

**Question 2: Grid Assessment**
> "When you're evaluating a new data center site, how do you assess whether the grid can support it? What information do you need from the utility?"

**Listen for**:
- ✅ Current process (formal study? informal inquiry?)
- ✅ Information gaps ("We never get enough detail upfront...")
- ✅ Timeline pressure ("We need answers in weeks, not months...")

**Question 3: Utility Relationships**
> "What's been your experience working with utilities on interconnection? Any patterns you've noticed?"

**Listen for**:
- ✅ Utility process frustration
- ✅ Slow turnaround times
- ✅ Willingness to work with tools that speed up utilities' decisions

**Question 4: Decision Making**
> "If you could accelerate grid assessment for your sites—say from 3 months to 3 weeks—how valuable would that be?"

**Listen for**:
- ✅ Quantifiable value (revenue, timeline)
- ✅ Decision authority ("I'd need finance to approve...")
- ✅ Budget availability ("We could allocate $X for faster permitting...")

### Qualification (5 min)

**Question 5: Solution Fit**
> "We work with utilities to speed up their capacity assessments, which means faster interconnection decisions for developers. Would that solve your bottleneck?"

**If YES**:

> "What would it take for you to be willing to partner on this with one of your target utilities?"

**Listen for**:
- ✅ Partnership interest
- ✅ Specific utilities they're engaged with
- ✅ Willingness to "warm intro" us to the utility

**If NO**:

> "What would actually solve the interconnection bottleneck for you?"

**Listen for**:
- ✅ Alternative solutions
- ✅ Real vs. polite objections

### Close (3-5 min)

**If Qualified**:
> "I'd like to understand your pipeline better. What utilities are you working with for your next 2-3 major facilities?"

**If Interested in Partnership**:
> "Could I set up a call with you and the utility's planning team to show how this speeds things up for both sides?"

**If Not Qualified**:
> "I appreciate the insights. Safe to stay in touch if the dynamics change?"

## Qualification Scorecard

**After each call, score 1-5 on these dimensions:**

### Problem Severity
- **1**: No real pain ("Our process works fine")
- **5**: Urgent ("This costs us millions annually")

### Budget Availability
- **1**: "We don't have budget"
- **5**: "We have budget allocated for this"

### Decision Timeline
- **1**: "Maybe sometime next year"
- **5**: "We need to move on this by Q3"

### Buying Committee Size
- **1**: "I'm the only decision maker"
- **5**: "Need to get 5 people aligned" (Note: 3-4 is ideal, indicates real org priority)

### Willingness to Pilot
- **1**: "Not interested in pilots"
- **5**: "Yes, let's schedule a demo this week"

### Likelihood to Buy
- **1**: "Very unlikely"
- **5**: "Very likely within 6 months"

**SCORING**:
- **20-25 points**: TIER 1 PROSPECT - Move to demo immediately
- **15-19 points**: TIER 2 PROSPECT - Add to nurture list, demo in 4 weeks
- **10-14 points**: TIER 3 PROSPECT - Keep informed, revisit in 6 months
- **<10 points**: NOT QUALIFIED - Archive, occasional check-ins

## Discovery Call Talking Points

**If asked "How much does this cost?"**:
> "For utilities, we're looking at $72K-$500K annually depending on scale and features. For a typical mid-size utility, think $150K-300K. Would that be in the ballpark of your IT/tools budget?"

**If asked "How long is the typical implementation?"**:
> "3-4 weeks from signup to running real scenarios. Most teams are productive on day 1 after 30 minutes of training."

**If asked "What's unique about GridVision?"**:
> "We model scenarios 40% faster than manual approaches, and integrate real load data with N-1 analysis to surface capacity risks others miss."

**If they mention a competitor**:
> "Yeah, [competitor] does X, but our advantage is Y. Happy to do a technical deep-dive if you want to compare."

## Red Flags (Disqualify)

🚩 "We're happy with our current process"  
→ No pain = no urgency. Archive for now.

🚩 "We don't have budget"  
→ Not prioritized. Check back in 12 months.

🚩 "We need enterprise support/customization"  
→ Too early stage for custom work. Tier 3 only.

🚩 "This sounds like [competitor]"  
→ They know alternatives. Competitive demo needed.

🚩 "Let me check with legal/compliance first"  
→ More complex sale. Real deal, but longer cycle.

---

## Green Flags (Prioritize)

✅ "This is exactly what we've been looking for"  
→ Product/market fit signal. Move fast.

✅ "We have budget allocated for this type of tool"  
→ Real budget. Likely to close.

✅ "When can we do a demo?"  
→ Genuine interest. Set demo immediately.

✅ "I want to involve [specific colleague] in the discussion"  
→ Building buying committee. Healthy sign.

✅ "Our CEO/board is pushing us on this"  
→ Executive mandate. Priority. Fast sale.
