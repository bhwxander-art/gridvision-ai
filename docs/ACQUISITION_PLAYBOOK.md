## Cold Outreach Sequence

### Email Sequence (5-touch over 4 weeks)

**Touch 1: Discovery Email** (Day 1)
- **Subject**: [Personalized hook based on company]
- **Goal**: Establish credibility, spark curiosity
- **Length**: 100 words max
- **CTA**: "Quick question"
- **Send time**: Tuesday 9 AM (recipient timezone)

**Template**:
```
Subject: {{company}} capacity planning—faster path forward

Hi {{contact_name}},

I noticed {{company}} is managing {{substations}} substations and growing 
{{growth_rate}}% annually. Capacity planning at your scale typically 
takes months.

We built GridVision specifically for this. Most utilities we talk to 
model scenarios 40% faster and surface capacity risks that manual 
planning misses.

Worth a quick conversation?

Best,
[Name]
```

**Touch 2: Follow-up 1** (Day 5)
- **Subject**: "Just following up..."
- **Goal**: Remind without being annoying
- **New angle**: Social proof (other utilities)
- **CTA**: "Let's find 20 minutes"

**Touch 3: Value Highlight** (Day 10)
- **Subject**: "{{company}} + GridVision ROI"
- **Goal**: Quantify value (send custom ROI)
- **Attachment**: ROI calculator link
- **CTA**: "See your number"

**Touch 4: Social Proof** (Day 15)
- **Subject**: "Other {{region}} utilities are..."
- **Goal**: FOMO + case study preview
- **Content**: Early pilot results, ROI validation
- **CTA**: "Join the conversation"

**Touch 5: Final Reach** (Day 21)
- **Subject**: "Last touch—{{company}} capacity planning"
- **Goal**: Final attempt, graceful exit
- **Content**: "Checking in one last time"
- **CTA**: "Let's chat or I'll step back"

## Phone/Meeting Cadence

### Discovery Call (20 minutes)

**Goal**: Qualify fit, understand pain, set up demo

**Questions**:
1. "What does your capacity planning process look like right now?"
2. "Where do you see the biggest bottlenecks?"
3. "How long does a typical scenario take to model?"
4. "What would accelerated planning enable?"
5. "Who would need to be involved in an evaluation?"

**Qualification Signals**:
- ✅ Planning takes >6 weeks
- ✅ Team is frustrated with speed
- ✅ Active interconnection pipeline
- ✅ Budget allocated for tools
- ✅ Executive sponsor identified

**Red Flags**:
- ❌ "We're happy with our current process"
- ❌ "No budget right now"
- ❌ No planning team (outsourced to consultant)

### Demo Call (45 minutes)

**Attendees**: Prospect + 1-2 planning team members

**Agenda**:
1. **Intro** (5 min): Who we are, why we built this
2. **Their situation** (5 min): Recap from discovery call
3. **Live demo** (25 min):
   - Login to demo tenant
   - Show 3-substation portfolio
   - Run real scenario (200 MW data center)
   - Show 3 upgrade options ranked by cost/benefit
   - Show portfolio analysis
   - Run ROI calculator with their profile
4. **Questions** (5 min)
5. **Next steps** (5 min): Pilot offer

**Demo Flow** (See PILOT_READINESS.md for full script)

**Success Signals**:
- ✅ "Can you show me on our data?"
- ✅ "How quickly can we get started?"
- ✅ "What would a pilot look like?"
- ✅ Asks about pricing

### Pilot Decision Call (30 minutes)

**Attendees**: Prospect sponsor + CFO/budget owner

**Agenda**:
1. **Pilot recap** (5 min): What we learned in pilot
2. **ROI confirmation** (10 min): Validate financial case
3. **Next steps** (5 min): Pricing, implementation timeline
4. **Signature** (5 min): Contract review

## Objection Handling

### "We're happy with our current process"

**Response**: "That's great to hear. Most planning teams we talk to are comfortable with their process, but frustrated with the speed. What if you could cut the time in half—what would that enable?"

**Path Forward**: Focus on time/speed, not replacement of existing process.

---

### "We don't have budget"

**Response**: "Budget is usually the real constraint. Here's the reality: most utilities find $72K in productivity gains from a single scenario. So it often pays for itself in the first project. Would it help if we worked backward from that?"

**Path Forward**: ROI case, reference a similar utility, offer phased implementation.

---

### "We need to evaluate other solutions first"

**Response**: "Totally fair. That said, most utilities that compare options choose us because we're 40% faster and $500K cheaper per year than the alternatives. Would it make sense to include us in the evaluation?"

**Path Forward**: Competitive positioning, offer feature comparison, suggest side-by-side demo.

---

### "We work with [Consultant/Systems Integrator]"

**Response**: "Many utilities do. We're actually built to integrate with existing planning workflows—not replace them. In fact, [Consultant] has been recommending us to other utilities. Would it help if we connected with them?"

**Path Forward**: Partner approach, offer integration, suggest three-way conversation.

## First Customer Playbook

### Characteristics of Ideal First Customer

✅ **Pain is urgent**: Capacity constraints, interconnection delays  
✅ **Budget is allocated**: Don't need to convince finance  
✅ **Quick decision cycle**: Executive sponsor involved  
✅ **Reference-worthy**: Willing to be case study  
✅ **Scalable**: $150K-500K ARR (not one-off)  

### Closing Tactics for First Customer

1. **Risk reversal**: "30-day pilot, full refund if not satisfied"
2. **Urgency**: "We're ramping usage features next quarter; early customer gets priority access"
3. **Executive sponsor**: "Let's get [VP] on a 15-min call to align on ROI"
4. **Simplify**: "Start with 1 substation, expand from there"
